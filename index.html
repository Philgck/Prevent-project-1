What is it and how can we help?

What it is

Prevent is the name given by the UK government to the safeguarding strategy designed to prevent vulnerable people from being drawn into extremism of any kind. It isn't just about policing people, and most referrals do not lead to any further police action. Often, referrals end up with the person being signposted to other services that can help them with their problems, such as health, mental health housing or education support.

Fundamentally, prevent is about engaging with communities to try and stop people from being drawn into extremism and look after both those who are vulnerable, and those around them. All referrals are handled sensitively and in confidence, which means reporting a concern will not make you look bad, and if it turns out that the person you were worried about is not actually at risk, it should not cause them any issues.

How you can help

Prevent is fundamentally about safeguarding. In the same ways you can become concerned about someone not looking after themselves, or for having poor mental health, you can end up concerned about radicalisation. Community engagement, talking to people, moderating online communities and knowing who to talk to about your concerns are all ways you can help preventing horrible things happening.

What it should not be

Despite the best of intentions, Prevent is not without controversy. There have been accusations of racial profiling, stereotyping, and over-reach with regards to the powers it gives officers. Some people think that it goes too far, and that some of the groups labelled as extremist should not be.

Glossary

Safeguarding

According to the NHS Safeguarding means protecting a citizen's health, wellbeing and human rights; enabling them to live free from harm, abuse and neglect. It is an integral part of providing high-quality health care. Safeguarding children, young people and adults is a collective responsibility.

Radicalisation

Radicalisation is the process by which a person comes to support or be involved in extremist ideologies. It is in of itself a form of harm.

Extremism

Extremism is the holding of extreme political or religious views. It is not illegal to hold these views, but it can be harmful if they lead to violence or other illegal activities. What is considered to be extremism can change over time, and different societies have different opinions on what counts as an extremist view.

Terrorism

Terrorism is the use of violence or the threat of violence to achieve a political goal. The European Union defines terrorism as acts that:

  • Intimidate a population,
  • Compel a government or international organisation to act or not act
  • Destabilise or destroy a country's political, constitutional, economic or social structures
